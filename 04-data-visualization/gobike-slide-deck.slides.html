Investigation Overview¶

Data Visualization Project Data Analysis Goals¶

  • Transportation is a important issue for many city planners. Providing alternative means of commuting and transportation is increasingly important to mitigate vehicle congestion and CO2 emissions.

  • Understanding residents' demand and use patterns for bicycles allows city administrators to effectively plan and manage bike share systems. The following analysis aims to provide an overview of year 2018 GoBike system, identify temporal ridership patterns, and to visualize geographic bike station use patterns.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset used in this project is Ford Gobike system data for 2018 which includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area. Bike share provides station-to-station trips around San Francsico to facilitate greater use of bicycles as a means of commute and to integrate biking into residents' daily life.

Age Distribution of GoBike Riders¶

  • There appear to be many bike riders over the age of 100 in San Francisco!
  • These ages are clearly incorrect but it is more important to leave this data as-is in order to know the total number of riders using the system. The age of every rider is less important for most planning purposes.
  • Many GoBike users prefered to keep their age anonymous or provided an incorrect age. Considering the increased awareness around data privacy issues, this is not surprising.

Geographical Station Demand Plot¶

Comparing maps of starting and ending station by demand can indicate traffic and use patterns. This is important information for city planners to design and manage the system to maximize participation.

  • Marker color and size depend on total station demand
  • Smaller and blue markers indicate less demand
  • larger and red markers indicate greater demand
